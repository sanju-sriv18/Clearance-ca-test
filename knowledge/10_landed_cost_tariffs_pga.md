## 1. COMPLETE LANDED COST COMPONENTS (US IMPORTS)

### 1.1 Product Cost (FOB Value)
The base purchase price of goods at the point of export (Free On Board). This is the starting point for all landed cost calculations and typically forms the basis of the customs value declaration on CBP Entry Summary (Form 7501).

### 1.2 International Freight
- **Ocean freight**: Full Container Load (FCL) or Less-than-Container Load (LCL) charges
- **Air freight**: Charged by dimensional or actual weight
- **Multimodal**: Combinations of ocean + rail/truck for door-to-door
- Freight costs are part of the dutiable value under the Transaction Value method (19 USC 1401a) only when they occur before importation. The US uses FOB valuation for customs purposes, meaning international freight is generally NOT added to the customs value for duty calculation (unlike CIF-based countries).

### 1.3 Insurance
- **Cargo insurance**: Typically 0.3%--0.5% of goods value for standard shipments; higher for fragile/high-value goods.
- Under US customs valuation, insurance costs incurred before importation are NOT included in the dutiable value (FOB basis), unlike CIF countries.

### 1.4 Customs Duties -- Column 1 General (MFN/NTR)
- Applied to goods from most countries maintaining Normal Trade Relations (NTR) with the US.
- Rates vary by HTSUS 8-digit classification, from 0% (duty-free) to over 30% for some goods.
- Determined by product classification under the HTSUS using GRI 1-6.

**IEEPA Reciprocal Tariffs (April 2025 onward):**
- Universal baseline: 10% on nearly all imports
- Country-specific rates: 10%-50%+ depending on bilateral trade deficit calculations
- Key negotiated rates: UK 10%, Japan 15%, South Korea 15%, Vietnam 20%, Philippines 19%, Indonesia 19%
- Canada: exempt (USMCA-qualifying goods 0%; non-USMCA goods subject to 35% fentanyl IEEPA tariff)
- Mexico: exempt from reciprocal tariff (USMCA-qualifying goods 0%; non-USMCA at 25% fentanyl IEEPA tariff)
- China: 10% reciprocal + 10% fentanyl IEEPA + Section 301 lists
- Exempt products: Section 232 goods, certain energy/mineral products, Canada/Mexico USMCA-qualifying goods
- Legal status: Federal Circuit ruled tariffs exceeded IEEPA authority (August 2025); Supreme Court arguments set for November 2025

**AD/CVD (Antidumping/Countervailing Duties):**
- Administered by Commerce Department (ITA) and USITC
- AD duties offset the dumping margin (difference between foreign market value and US price)
- CVD duties offset foreign government subsidies
- Rates determined through investigation; can range from 0% to over 300%
- Annual administrative reviews reassess rates each anniversary of the order
- Cash deposits collected at entry; final assessment after administrative review
- 657+ active orders as of 2022, affecting imports from 59 countries
- $30.2B in goods subject to AD/CVD in FY2021

**Section 201 (Safeguard):**
- Solar panels/cells: 14% (February 7, 2025 -- February 6, 2026); expires February 7, 2026
- Tariff-rate quota: 5 GW for cells (expandable by 7.5 GW)
- Bifacial panel exclusion revoked (June 2024)
- Washing machines: Expired February 2023 (no longer in effect)

### 1.6 Merchandise Processing Fee (MPF)
- **Rate**: 0.3464% ad valorem of entered value
- **Formal entries** (goods valued >$2,500):
  - FY2025 (before Oct 1, 2025): Min $32.71 / Max $634.62
  - FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025 onward): Min $33.58 / Max $651.50
- **Informal entries** (goods valued <$2,500): FY2026 min $2.69
- Applied to the FOB value of merchandise (not including duty, freight, or insurance)
- **Exemptions**: Goods qualifying under certain FTAs (USMCA, KORUS, Chile, Singapore, Australia, Peru, Colombia, Panama, Israel, Bahrain, Oman, Morocco, Jordan, CAFTA-DR)
- Legal basis: 19 USC 58c; COBRA fees adjusted annually per FAST Act

### 1.7 Harbor Maintenance Fee (HMF)
- **Rate**: 0.125% of declared customs value
- **Applies to**: Ocean freight imports only (not air, truck, or rail)
- **No minimum or maximum cap**
- Applies to cargo loaded/unloaded at Army Corps of Engineers-designated ports
- Also applies to FTZ admissions and cruise passenger ticket values
- Exception: Cargo transported via ocean to a Canadian port and then routed into the US
- Legal basis: Water Resources Development Act of 1986; funds the Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund
- Collected by CBP

### 1.8 Customs Broker Fees
- Typical range: $150--$500+ per entry depending on complexity
- Additional charges for classification research, PGA filings, post-entry amendments
- Brokers must be licensed by CBP (19 CFR Part 111)

### 1.9 ISF Filing Fees
- **Applies to**: All ocean vessel shipments bound for the US
- Must be filed at least **24 hours before vessel loading** at foreign port
- Broker filing fees: typically **$30--$80** per filing
- ISF bond cost: ~$50--$70 for single-transaction
- Amendment fees: $20--$40 per amendment
- **Penalties**: Up to $5,000 per violation (late, incomplete, or inaccurate); maximum $10,000 per shipment
- First violations may be mitigated to $1,000--$2,000
- C-TPAT members receive up to 50% penalty mitigation
- 10 data elements from importer + 2 from carrier

### 1.10 Container Drayage
- Local trucking from port to warehouse/destination
- Varies by market: typically $300--$1,500+ depending on distance and chassis availability

### 1.11 Terminal Handling Charges (THC)
- Port fees for unloading/handling containers at marine terminals
- Destination THC (DTHC): typically $200--$600 per container at US ports

### 1.12 Demurrage and Detention
- **Demurrage**: Charges for container sitting at the marine terminal beyond free time (typically 3-5 free days); $75--$400+/day escalating
- **Detention**: Charges for container held by consignee beyond free time after pickup; similar escalating rate structure
- Subject to FMC (Federal Maritime Commission) regulation

### 1.13 Warehouse/Storage
- If goods require bonded warehouse storage (CBP-approved): $0.50--$2.00+ per sq ft/month
- General warehouse rates vary by region and facility

### 1.14 Last-Mile Delivery
- Final delivery from port/warehouse to end destination
- Varies significantly by mode, distance, and shipment size

### 1.15 Bond Costs

**Continuous Bond (Annual):**
- Covers unlimited entries at all US ports for 12 months
- Minimum bond amount: **$50,000**
- Calculated as 10% of annual duties/taxes/fees, rounded up to nearest $10,000
- Over $1M: round to nearest $100,000
- **Premium** (what you actually pay): 1%--15% of bond amount
- Typical cost: $250--$750+/year for a $50K bond
- Covers ISF filing obligations

**Single Entry Bond:**
- Covers one shipment at one specified port
- Bond amount: value of goods + duties + taxes + fees
- For PGA-regulated goods: 3x the value
- Premium: $3--$5 per $1,000 of bond amount; minimum ~$100
- Does NOT cover ISF (separate ISF bond needed: ~$50--$70)

### 1.16 Insurance
- **Cargo insurance**: 0.3%--0.5% of goods value (all-risk marine cargo)
- **Liability insurance**: Varies by industry and product risk profile
- **Errors & omissions**: For importers, covers customs compliance mistakes

## 2. US TARIFF STRUCTURE IN DETAIL

### 2.1 Column Structure

**Column 1 -- General (MFN/NTR Rates):**
- Applies to goods from all countries with which the US maintains Normal Trade Relations
- This covers virtually every country except Cuba, North Korea, Russia (for certain goods), and Belarus
- Rates are negotiated through WTO/GATT rounds
- These are the "baseline" duty rates

**Column 1 -- Special (Preferential Rates):**
- Reduced or zero-duty rates under FTAs or trade preference programs
- Indicated by Special Program Indicators (SPIs) in the HTSUS
- Key SPIs:
  - **S** -- USMCA (formerly CA/MX for NAFTA)
  - **KR** -- Korea (KORUS)
  - **IL** -- Israel
  - **AU** -- Australia
  - **CL** -- Chile
  - **SG** -- Singapore
  - **PE** -- Peru
  - **CO** -- Colombia
  - **PA** -- Panama
  - **P/P+** -- DR-CAFTA
  - **BH** -- Bahrain
  - **MA** -- Morocco
  - **OM** -- Oman
  - **JO** -- Jordan
  - **A/A+** -- GSP (Generalized System of Preferences) -- note: GSP expired December 31, 2020 and has not been renewed as of 2025

**Column 2 (Non-NTR Rates):**
- Significantly higher, punitive rates
- Currently applies to: **Cuba, North Korea**
- Rates can be 10-20x higher than Column 1 General
- Column 2 goods are exempt from reciprocal tariffs (already subject to highest rates)

### 2.2 Duty Rate Types

**Ad Valorem:**
- Percentage of customs value (e.g., "6.5%")
- Most common type (~83% of dutiable 8-digit tariff lines)
- Applied to transaction value under 19 USC 1401a

**Specific:**
- Fixed amount per unit of quantity (e.g., "$0.20/kg" or "$1.50/dozen")
- Based on weight, volume, count, or other physical measure
- Value-independent -- same duty regardless of goods' price

**Compound:**
- Combination of ad valorem + specific (e.g., "5% + $0.10/kg")
- Common in agriculture, textiles, footwear
- Both components are calculated and added together

**Mixed/Alternative:**
- Conditional choice between ad valorem and specific, subject to ceiling/floor
- Example: "The higher of 5% or $0.15/kg"

### 2.3 HTSUS Structure

**Sections (I -- XXII):** Broadest grouping by type of good (e.g., Section XI = Textiles and Textile Articles)

**Chapters (01--99):** 99 chapters organized by commodity type
- Chapters 01--97: Commodity chapters
- Chapter 98: Special classification provisions
- Chapter 99: Temporary modifications (trade remedies, tariff actions)

**Headings (4-digit):** e.g., 8471 = Automatic data processing machines

**Subheadings (6-digit):** International level (HS) -- e.g., 8471.30 = Portable automatic data processing machines

**US Subheading (8-digit):** Rate line level -- duty rate determined here (e.g., 8471.30.01)

**Statistical Suffix (digits 9-10):** e.g., 8471.30.01.00 -- Used for trade data collection only; no legal significance for duty rates

### 2.4 General Rules of Interpretation (GRI 1--6)

**GRI 1 -- Terms of Headings and Section/Chapter Notes:**
Classification is determined first by the terms of the headings and any relative Section or Chapter Notes. Section titles, chapter titles, and subchapter titles are for reference only. GRI 1 takes precedence over all other rules.

**GRI 2(a) -- Incomplete, Unfinished, Unassembled Goods:**
An incomplete or unfinished article is classified as the complete article if it has the essential character of the finished article. Also covers goods presented unassembled or disassembled.

**GRI 2(b) -- Mixtures and Combinations:**
A reference to a material includes mixtures or combinations of that material with other materials. Allows classification in multiple possible headings, leading to GRI 3 if ambiguous.

**GRI 3(a) -- Most Specific Description:**
When goods appear classifiable under two or more headings, the most specific description takes precedence over the more general.

**GRI 3(b) -- Essential Character (Sets):**
Composite goods, mixtures, and goods put up in sets for retail sale are classified by the component that gives them their essential character.

**GRI 3(c) -- Last in Numerical Order:**
If 3(a) and 3(b) fail, classify under the heading that occurs last in numerical order.

**GRI 4 -- Most Akin:**
Goods that cannot be classified under GRI 1-3 are classified under the heading for goods to which they are most similar.

**GRI 5 -- Containers and Packing:**
- 5(a): Specially shaped containers (camera cases, instrument cases) are classified with the article when of a kind normally sold therewith.
- 5(b): Packing materials are classified with the goods unless clearly suitable for repetitive use.

**GRI 6 -- Subheading Classification:**
Classification at the subheading level within a heading is governed by GRI 1-5 applied mutatis mutandis. Only subheadings at the same level are comparable.

### 2.5 Additional US Notes
- Found at the beginning of specific HTSUS chapters
- Provide US-specific definitions, limitations, and special provisions
- Often define quota quantities, tariff-rate quotas, and product-specific rules
- Have legal force for classification and duty rate determination

### 2.6 Statistical Suffixes (10-digit level)
- Digits 9-10 of the HTSUS code
- Administered by the US Census Bureau for trade statistics
- No impact on duty rates or legal classification
- Required on all Entry Summary filings (CBP Form 7501)
- Example: 0902.10.90.15 -- the ".15" suffix distinguishes certified organic green tea from non-organic within the same 8-digit rate line

**Structure:**
- Universal baseline: 10% on imports from nearly all countries
- Country-specific "reciprocal" rates: higher rates for 57 countries
- Fentanyl-related IEEPA tariffs on China (10%), Canada (35% non-USMCA), Mexico (25% non-USMCA)

| South Korea | 15% |
| Vietnam | 20% |
| Philippines | 19% |
| Indonesia | 19% |
| Canada | Exempt from reciprocal; 35% fentanyl (non-USMCA) |
| Mexico | Exempt from reciprocal; 25% fentanyl (non-USMCA) |
| Most other countries | 10% baseline |

**Exemptions from IEEPA Reciprocal:**
- Section 232 goods (steel, aluminum, autos, copper)
- Certain energy and mineral products
- Canada and Mexico (USMCA-qualifying goods)
- Column 2 countries (already at highest rates)
- Products listed in Annex II of executive orders

**Tariff Stacking:** IEEPA tariffs are cumulative with Column 1 General duties, Section 301, AD/CVD, and Section 201.

**Legal Status:** Federal Circuit ruled tariffs exceeded IEEPA authority (August 29, 2025); Supreme Court hearing November 2025, decision expected 2026.

### 3.4 AD/CVD Process

**Initiation:**
1. US industry files petition with ITA and USITC simultaneously
2. ITA has 20 days to determine whether to initiate investigation
3. Self-initiation by Commerce also possible

**Investigation Timeline:**
- USITC preliminary injury determination: ~45 days
- ITA preliminary determination: ~160 days (AD) / ~85 days (CVD)
- ITA final determination: 75 days (CVD) / 75-135 days (AD) after preliminary
- USITC final injury determination: ~45 days after ITA final

**Duty Assessment:**
- Preliminary affirmative determination triggers suspension of liquidation and cash deposit requirement
- Final affirmative determinations from both ITA and USITC result in AD/CVD order
- Cash deposits collected at entry; final duties assessed after administrative review

**Annual Administrative Review:**
- Available each anniversary month of the order publication
- Interested parties request review via ACCESS system (19 CFR 351.213)
- Review period typically covers prior 12-month period
- If no review requested: duties assessed at cash deposit rate
- If reviewed: new rates calculated based on actual transaction data
- Final results typically issued within 18 months of initiation

## 4. PARTNER GOVERNMENT AGENCIES (PGAs) AND THEIR REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 FDA (Food and Drug Administration)

**Products Regulated:**
- Human food (Chapters 02-23 and others)
- Animal feed (various chapters)
- Human drugs (Chapter 30)
- Biologics (Chapter 30)
- Medical devices (Chapters 84, 85, 90)
- Cosmetics (Chapter 33)
- Radiation-emitting electronic products (Chapters 84, 85, 90)
- Tobacco products (Chapter 24)

**Key Requirements:**
- Prior Notice required for all food shipments (submitted via PNSI or ACE)
- Foreign facility registration (FDA Food Facility Registration)
- FDA Establishment Identifier (FEI) for manufacturers
- Product Code classification (Industry Code = first 2 digits)
- Intended Use Codes (IUCs)
- Affirmations of Compliance (AofCs) for specific product types
- Drug listing and registration for pharmaceutical products
- 510(k) clearance or PMA approval for medical devices
- FSMA compliance (Foreign Supplier Verification Program)

**ACE Filing:**
- PGA Message Set required when HTS code carries "FD" flag
- FDA ACE Supplemental Guide (current version 2.5.12)
- Record identifiers: PG01 through PG30
- Key data: manufacturer FEI, product code, intended use, AofCs

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing or invalid Prior Notice
- Unregistered foreign facility
- Labeling violations
- Detention without physical examination (DWPE) based on import alerts
- Adulteration or misbranding under FD&C Act sections 801(a), 402, 403

### 4.2 USDA/APHIS (Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service)

**Products Regulated:**
- Live plants and plant products (Chapters 06-14)
- Fruits and vegetables (Chapters 07, 08)
- Seeds (Chapter 12)
- Cut flowers (Chapter 06)
- Wood and wood products (Chapter 44) -- WPM/ISPM 15
- Live animals (Chapter 01)
- Animal products (Chapters 02, 05, 15, 16)
- Meat, poultry, egg products (Chapters 02, 04, 16) -- via FSIS

**Key Requirements:**
- Import permits via APHIS eFile system (some products)
- Phytosanitary certificates from country of origin
- ISPM 15 treatment/marking for wood packaging material
- APHIS health certificates for live animals
- Lacey Act declarations for plants/plant products
- FSIS equivalence determination for meat/poultry/egg exporting countries

**ACE Filing:**
- APHIS Core Message Set for regulated items
- HTS codes flagged AQ1 (may be required) or AQX (warning)
- LPCOs (Licenses, Permits, Certificates, Other Documents) must be referenced
- DIS codes: APH01 (foreign government certificates), APH02 (supporting documents)

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Pest detection during inspection
- Missing or invalid phytosanitary certificate
- Products from countries without FSIS equivalence
- Non-compliant wood packaging material
- Quarantine pest interception

### 4.3 EPA (Environmental Protection Agency)

**Products Regulated:**
- Chemical substances under TSCA (various chapters, particularly 28, 29, 38)
- Motor vehicles (Chapter 87) -- emissions standards
- Non-road engines (Chapters 84, 87)
- Pesticides and herbicides (Chapter 38)
- Ozone-depleting substances (Chapter 29, 38)
- Fuels and fuel additives

**Key Requirements:**
- **TSCA Section 13 Import Certification**: Positive or negative certification required
  - Positive: substance is TSCA-compliant
  - Negative: substance is not subject to TSCA (e.g., FDA-regulated)
  - No certification needed for articles (unless specific TSCA rule applies)
- TSCA Title VI certification for composite wood products (formaldehyde standards)
- EPA Form 3520-1 for vehicle/engine imports (emissions compliance)
- Pre-Manufacture Notice (PMN) for new chemicals not on TSCA Inventory (90 days prior)

**ACE Filing:**
- HTS codes flagged EP7 for TSCA
- PGA Message Set or DIS upload (tag: EPA06)
- ~450 additional HTS codes flagged for composite wood products
- EPA Supplemental Guidelines (CATAIR)

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing TSCA certification
- New chemical without PMN
- Non-compliant vehicle emissions
- Composite wood products exceeding formaldehyde limits

### 4.4 CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission)

**Products Regulated:**
- Children's products (toys, cribs, clothing) -- multiple chapters
- General consumer products (carpets, apparel, lighters, bike helmets)
- Products subject to mandatory safety standards under CPSA, FHSA, FFA, PPPA

**Key Requirements:**
- **Children's Product Certificate (CPC)**: Required for products designed for children 12 and under
  - Must be based on third-party testing by CPSC-accepted laboratory
  - Covers: lead content, phthalates, small parts, flammability, ASTM F963, etc.
- **General Certificate of Conformity (GCC)**: Required for regulated general-use products
  - First-party testing acceptable (no third-party lab required)
  - Covers: mattress flammability (16 CFR 1633), carpet flammability, cigarette lighter standards, etc.

**ACE Filing (New eFiling Rule effective July 8, 2026):**
- Mandatory eFiling of certificates of compliance via ACE PGA Message Set
- Applies to ALL imported products subject to mandatory CPSA safety standards
- Includes de minimis shipments
- Importer of Record responsible for filing

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing or false certificate of compliance
- Lead content exceeding limits in children's products
- Small parts hazard in products for children under 3
- Non-compliant product detained at port by Office of Import Surveillance

### 4.5 FCC (Federal Communications Commission)

**Products Regulated:**
- Radio frequency (RF) devices (Chapters 84, 85)
- Telecommunications equipment
- Computing devices, Wi-Fi routers, Bluetooth devices
- Any device capable of emitting RF energy

**Key Requirements:**
- **Certification**: Required for devices with greatest interference potential
  - Issued by FCC-recognized Telecommunication Certification Body (TCB)
  - FCC ID required, listed in FCC database
- **Supplier's Declaration of Conformity (SDoC)**: Self-declaration by responsible party (must be US-based)
- Import conditions under 47 CFR 2.1204 (11 conditions for lawful importation)

**ACE Filing:**
- FCC Form 740 (Declaration of Conformity) required at entry
- HTS codes flagged for FCC jurisdiction

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- No valid FCC equipment authorization (no FCC ID)
- Device marketed/imported without required certification
- Unauthorized RF device (jammers, unauthorized boosters)

### 4.6 TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau)

**Products Regulated:**
- Distilled spirits (Chapter 22, 2208)
- Wine (Chapter 22, 2204)
- Beer/malt beverages (Chapter 22, 2203)
- Tobacco products (Chapter 24)
- Processed tobacco

**Key Requirements:**
- **Federal Basic Importer's Permit** (TTB F 5100.24) required for alcohol importers
- **Certificate of Label Approval (COLA)** for each unique product/label
- Certificate of age/origin for certain wines and spirits
- Tobacco import permit for business importers
- Excise tax payment at entry
- CBMA (Craft Beverage Modernization Act) tax rate claims via ACE

**ACE Filing:**
- TTB ACE Filing Guide for commodity-specific data elements
- CBMA tax rates submitted via ACE entry summary
- Effective Jan 1, 2023: pay full rate to CBP, then claim CBMA refund from TTB

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing or expired COLA
- No valid importer's permit
- Incorrect excise tax calculation
- Label violations (health warning, alcohol content, origin)

### 4.7 Fish & Wildlife Service (USFWS)

**Products Regulated:**
- Wildlife and wildlife products (Chapters 01, 05, 41, 42, 43, 97)
- Fish and shellfish (Chapter 03, 16)
- Plants protected under CITES (Chapters 06, 12, 13, 14, 44)
- Endangered species products (ivory, exotic skins, etc.)

**Key Requirements:**
- **CITES permit** for species listed in Appendices I, II, III
- **Lacey Act declaration** for plants and plant products (Form 3-177)
- Import/export through designated ports only (certain exceptions)
- USFWS Form 3-177 (Declaration for Importation/Exportation of Fish or Wildlife)
- Endangered Species Act (ESA) permits for listed species

**ACE Filing:**
- USFWS PGA Message Set required for flagged HTS codes
- Must declare species, country of origin, purpose of import

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing CITES permit for protected species
- Importation through non-designated port
- False Lacey Act declaration
- Products from species prohibited from commercial trade (Appendix I)

### 4.8 DOT/NHTSA (National Highway Traffic Safety Administration)

**Products Regulated:**
- Motor vehicles (Chapter 87)
- Motor vehicle equipment (tires, lighting, glazing, seat belts)
- Car seats, motorcycle helmets

**Key Requirements:**
- **HS-7 Declaration Form** filed at entry
- Vehicle must comply with all applicable Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS)
- Manufacturer's certification label required on vehicle
- Vehicles <25 years old must meet all FMVSS
- Vehicles manufactured after Sept 1, 1978 must also meet bumper standard
- Model year 1987+ must meet theft-prevention standard
- **Nonconforming vehicles**: Must use NHTSA-approved Registered Importer (RI) + DOT Conformance Bond (1.5x dutiable value)
- 25-year exemption for classic/antique vehicles

**ACE Filing:**
- DOT/NHTSA data elements filed via ACE entry

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- Missing HS-7 form
- No certification label
- Nonconforming vehicle without RI contract and conformance bond

### 4.9 DEA (Drug Enforcement Administration)

**Products Regulated:**
- Schedule I-V controlled substances (Chapters 29, 30, 13)
- Listed chemicals (ephedrine, pseudoephedrine, phenylpropanolamine)
- Tableting and encapsulating machines

**Key Requirements:**
- **DEA Registration** as importer (annual)
- **DEA Form 357** (Import Permit) for Schedule I/II substances and all narcotic drugs (Schedules III-V)
- Import declarations for non-narcotic Schedule III-V substances (15 days advance)
- Annual import quotas for Schedule I/II substances
- Permits valid for maximum 6 months

**ACE Filing:**
- DEA import data submitted through ACE
- DEA Form 6A transmitted electronically to CBP

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- No valid DEA registration
- Missing import permit or declaration
- Exceeding quota allocation
- Import from proscribed country

### 4.10 ATF (Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives)

**Products Regulated:**
- Firearms (Chapter 93)
- Ammunition (Chapter 93)
- Explosives (Chapter 36)
- Defense articles/implements of war

**Key Requirements:**
- **Federal Firearms License (FFL)**: Type 08 or Type 11 for commercial importation
- **ATF Form 6 (Part I)**: Application and Permit for Importation (processing time: 4-6 weeks)
- Sporting purpose requirement for firearms (18 USC 925(d)(3))
- Arms Export Control Act (AECA) registration for commercial importers
- ATF Form 4590 point system for pistol importability

**ACE Filing:**
- CBP copy of Form 6A transmitted electronically via ACE

**Common Reasons for Holds/Refusals:**
- No approved ATF Form 6
- Firearm fails sporting purpose test
- Attempt to import from proscribed country
- No valid FFL

## 5. FREE TRADE AGREEMENTS ACTIVE FOR THE US

The US has **14 FTAs with 20 countries**:

### 5.1 USMCA (Canada, Mexico) -- Effective July 1, 2020
- **SPI Code**: S
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + Regional Value Content (RVC)
  - Transaction Value method: RVC = ((TV - VNM) / TV) x 100
  - Net Cost method: RVC = ((NC - VNM) / NC) x 100
- **De minimis**: 10% of adjusted value for non-originating materials
- **Automotive**: 75% RVC, 40-45% labor value content ($16/hr), 70% North American steel/aluminum
- **Certificate**: 9 required data elements; no prescribed form; importer, exporter, or producer may certify
- **Blanket period**: Up to 12 months for identical goods
- **Recordkeeping**: 5 years (US), 6 years (Mexico/Canada)
- **MPF exempt**: Yes
- **Key categories**: Automotive (stringent rules), textiles (yarn-forward), agriculture (TRQs), digital trade

### 5.2 US-Israel FTA -- Effective September 1, 1985
- **SPI Code**: IL
- **Rules of Origin**: Substantial transformation + 35% value-added (cost of materials + direct processing costs)
- **Certificate**: Invoice declaration (since 2017 amendment; eliminated separate CO form for exports to Israel)
- **MPF exempt**: Yes (19 CFR 24.23)
- **Key categories**: Manufactured goods (duty-free since 1995), some agricultural products with tariff preferences

### 5.3 US-Australia FTA (AUSFTA) -- Effective January 1, 2005
- **SPI Code**: AU
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + RVC (product-specific in General Note 28(n))
- **De minimis**: 10% of adjusted value (non-textiles)
- **Certificate**: No specific certificate required; importer makes claim at entry
- **MPF exempt**: Yes
- **Key categories**: Agriculture (beef TRQs), manufactured goods, services

### 5.4 US-Chile FTA -- Effective January 1, 2004
- **SPI Code**: CL
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + RVC (product-specific)
- **De minimis**: 10%
- **Certificate**: Certificate of origin required
- **MPF exempt**: Yes
- **Key categories**: Wine, fresh fruit, copper, manufactured goods

### 5.5 US-Singapore FTA -- Effective January 1, 2004
- **SPI Code**: SG
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + RVC (product-specific)
- **De minimis**: 10%
- **Certificate**: Certification of origin
- **MPF exempt**: Yes (including Integrated Sourcing Initiative goods)
- **Key categories**: Electronics, pharmaceuticals, petrochemicals

### 5.6 US-Korea FTA (KORUS) -- Effective March 15, 2012
- **SPI Code**: KR
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + RVC; wholly obtained/produced or tariff shift/RVC
- **De minimis**: 10% (exceptions for textiles and certain goods)
- **Certificate**: Certification by importer, exporter, or producer
- **MPF exempt**: Yes
- **Key categories**: Automotive, electronics, textiles, agriculture (beef, pork TRQs)

### 5.7 US-Colombia TPA -- Effective May 15, 2012
- **SPI Code**: CO
- **Rules of Origin**: Tariff shift + RVC (product-specific)
- **De minimis**: 10%
- **Certificate**: Certification of origin
- **MPF exempt**: Yes (19 CFR 24.23(c)(14))

### 5.8 US-Panama TPA -- Effective October 31, 2012

- **Certificate**: Certification of origin

## 6. DE MINIMIS RULES

### 6.1 Section 321 Threshold
- **Statutory authority**: 19 USC 1321(a)(2)(C) (Section 321 of Tariff Act of 1930)
- **Threshold**: $800 per person, per day
- Raised from $200 to $800 by Trade Facilitation and Trade Enforcement Act of 2015 (TFTEA)
- Applied to: One shipment per person, per day
- **FY2024 volume**: 1.36 billion de minimis shipments processed

### 6.2 Interaction with Section 301/232/IEEPA

**Pre-May 2025:**
- De minimis shipments from all countries (including China) were generally exempt from duties, including Section 301, 232, and IEEPA tariffs
- This was identified as a significant compliance gap

**May 2, 2025 -- China/Hong Kong:**
- De minimis exemption suspended for goods from China and Hong Kong
- All applicable duties now apply regardless of value
- Entry Type 86 no longer accepted for Chinese goods
- Postal shipments: flat per-item duty ($100 initially, increased to $200 on June 1, 2025)

**August 29, 2025 -- All Countries:**
- De minimis exemption suspended universally
- Entry Type 86 no longer accepted for any goods
- All shipments regardless of value subject to full duties, taxes, and customs procedures
- Postal shipment duties: $80-$200 per item depending on country IEEPA rate tier

### 6.3 Entry Type 86 (De Minimis with PGA Requirements)
- **Created**: Part of CBP Section 321 Data Pilot and Entry Type 86 Test
- **Purpose**: Allowed formal entry processing for de minimis shipments that required PGA data (e.g., FDA Prior Notice)
- **Status**: No longer accepted as of August 29, 2025
- Previously provided mechanism to file PGA data while still claiming de minimis duty exemption

### 6.4 Legislative Elimination
- **"Megabill" (signed July 4, 2025)**: Legislatively eliminates de minimis entry privilege for commercial shipments from all countries
- **Effective date**: July 1, 2027
- **New penalties**: $5,000 first violation; $10,000 subsequent violations for misuse of de minimis process (effective August 3, 2025)
- This makes the executive suspension permanent through legislation

### 6.5 Proposed Changes (Rulemaking)
- **January 14, 2025 NPRM**: Additional reporting requirements for low-value imports
- **January 17, 2025 NPRM**: Proposed eliminating Section 321 eligibility for goods subject to Section 301/232/201 tariffs
- Both proposed rules were partly superseded by executive actions and the Megabill legislation
