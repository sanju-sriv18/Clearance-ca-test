# EXPORT CLEARANCE: COMPREHENSIVE GLOBAL REFERENCE

## 1. US EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 1.1 AES (Automated Export System) / ACE Export

The Automated Export System (AES) is the export component of the Automated Commercial Environment (ACE). It is the system for collecting, processing, and storing Electronic Export Information (EEI) from persons or entities exporting goods from the United States, Puerto Rico, or the U.S. Virgin Islands.

**When EEI Filing Is Required:**
- Shipments where the value of the commodity classified under each individual Schedule B number exceeds **$2,500**
- Any shipment requiring a **validated export license**, regardless of value
- All shipments containing **hazardous materials**, regardless of value
- Shipments sent under a **State Department license** (DSP-5, DSP-61, DSP-73, etc.)
- Shipments from the U.S. mainland to **Puerto Rico or the Virgin Islands** if meeting the above criteria

**Filing Timelines:**
- **Non-ITAR shipments**: EEI must be filed no later than **2 hours** before exportation
- **ITAR shipments**: EEI must be filed no later than **8 hours** before exportation
- **Postdeparture Filing Program**: Previously approved companies may report EEI within **5 calendar days** after the date of export. Shipments requiring a license may only use postdeparture filing when the appropriate licensing agency has granted authorization

**Filing Methods:**
1. **AESDirect** -- the free, web-based portal operated by the Census Bureau
2. Custom AES software developed using the AESTIR (AES Trade Interface Requirements)
3. Purchased commercial software from certified vendors
4. Filing through an **authorized agent** (freight forwarder, customs broker)
5. An FPPI (Foreign Principal Party in Interest) can **only** use an authorized agent in a routed transaction

**Schedule B Classification:**
- Schedule B numbers are **10-digit** codes used exclusively for U.S. exports
- The first **6 digits** are identical to the international HS code; the remaining 4 digits are US-specific
- There are approximately **9,000** Schedule B codes (compared to ~19,000 HTSUS import codes)
- Schedule B numbers are administered by the **U.S. Census Bureau, Foreign Trade Division**
- Incorrect classification can result in fines, penalties, and shipment delays

**Exemptions from EEI Filing (Key NOEEI Citations):**
- **NOEEI 30.37(a)**: Low-value shipments -- commodities valued at **$2,500 or less** per individual Schedule B number. Cannot be used if the commodity requires an export license, is subject to ITAR, contains rough diamonds, or is destined for Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Sudan, or Syria
- **NOEEI 30.36**: Shipments to **Canada** (unless goods require an export license, contain rough diamonds, or are ITAR-controlled)
- **NOEEI 30.37(b)**: **Tools of trade** and their containers for use by employees abroad
- **NOEEI 30.37(f)**: Technology and software (as defined in EAR 15 CFR 772) not requiring an export license
- **NOEEI 30.37(g)**: Books, maps, pamphlets to foreign libraries or government establishments
- **NOEEI 30.37(h)**: Gift parcels and humanitarian donations under License Exception GFT
- **NOEEI 30.37(i)**: Diplomatic pouches
- **NOEEI 30.37(k)**: Inter-plant correspondence and company business records
- When exempt, the exporter must annotate **"NOEEI"** followed by the FTR citation on the commercial document (air waybill, bill of lading, invoice)

**Record Retention:** All parties must retain documents pertaining to the shipment for **5 years** from the date of export. Other agencies may require retention beyond 5 years.

**Penalties:** Failure to file, late filing, or submission of incorrect information can lead to substantial fines per violation, shipment delays, and loss of export privileges.

Sources: [CBP AES Technical Information](https://www.cbp.gov/trade/technical-documentation/aes), [Census Bureau AES](https://www.census.gov/foreign-trade/aes/index.html), [15 CFR 30.2](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/15/30.2), [Trade.gov AES Guide](https://www.trade.gov/filing-your-export-shipments-through-automated-export-system-aes), [15 CFR 30.37 Exemptions](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-15/subtitle-B/chapter-I/part-30/subpart-D/section-30.37)

### 1.2 Export Controls

#### EAR (Export Administration Regulations) -- Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS)

The EAR (15 C.F.R. Parts 730-774) is administered by BIS within the U.S. Department of Commerce. A license is required to export, reexport, or transfer (in-country) certain controlled items.

**ECCN (Export Control Classification Number):**
- ECCNs are **five-character alpha-numeric designations** on the Commerce Control List (CCL)
- Format example: **3A001** -- the first digit = category (0-9), the letter = product group (A-E), the remaining digits = reason for control and sequence
- Product groups: A = Equipment/Assemblies/Components, B = Test/Inspection/Production Equipment, C = Materials, D = Software, E = Technology
- Categories: 0 = Nuclear, 1 = Special Materials/Processing, 2 = Materials Processing, 3 = Electronics, 4 = Computers, 5 = Telecommunications/Information Security, 6 = Sensors/Lasers, 7 = Navigation/Avionics, 8 = Marine, 9 = Aerospace/Propulsion

**EAR99 Designation:** Items subject to the EAR but not described in any ECCN on the CCL. EAR99 items generally do not require a license but **may** require one if destined for a prohibited end user, end use, or destination of concern.

**License Determination Process:**
1. Determine if the item is subject to the EAR
2. Classify the item on the CCL (find the ECCN)
3. Check the **Commerce Country Chart** (Supplement No. 1 to Part 738) against the Reasons for Control
4. Screen all parties against restricted party lists (Entity List, Denied Persons List, Unverified List, SDN List)
5. Check for prohibited end uses (nuclear, missile, chemical/biological weapons, military-intelligence under 744.21/744.22)
6. Determine if a License Exception applies
7. If no exception applies, submit a license application via **SNAP-R** (Simplified Network Application Process Redesign)

**Key License Exceptions (Part 740):**
- **LVS (Limited Value Shipments, 740.3)**: Exports to Country Group B destinations where net value does not exceed the dollar limit specified in the ECCN's LVS paragraph
- **TMP (Temporary Imports/Exports/Reexports, 740.9)**: Temporary movement of items, kits of replacement parts, items for exhibition/demonstration
- **TSR (Technology and Software Under Restriction, 740.6)**: List-based exception available only when the ECCN specifically authorizes it
- **RPL (Replacement of Parts and Equipment, 740.10)**: One-for-one replacement of parts, components, accessories, and attachments
- **STA (Strategic Trade Authorization, 740.20)**: For exports to Country Group A:5 destinations of items eligible under specific ECCNs
- **ENC (Encryption, 740.17)**: For encryption commodities, software, and technology
- **GOV (Governments and International Organizations, 740.11)**
- **BAG (Baggage, 740.14)**
- **GFT (Gift Parcels and Humanitarian Donations, 740.12)**
- **IEC (Implemented Export Controls, 740.24)**: New in September 2024, for items in 900-series ECCNs destined for countries that have adopted comparable controls

**Key 2024 Developments:**
- September 2024 Interim Final Rule added new ECCNs for **semiconductor, quantum computing, and additive manufacturing** items
- New 900-series ECCNs created for items controlled on national security and regional stability grounds
- April 2024 rules updated semiconductor/supercomputer controls, including new ECCN 4A090.b for advanced computing items

Sources: [BIS Licensing](https://www.bis.gov/licensing), [Commerce Control List](https://www.bis.gov/regulations/ear/738), [BIS ECCN Guide](https://www.trade.gov/how-do-i-determine-my-export-control-classification-number-eccn), [Part 740 License Exceptions](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-15/subtitle-B/chapter-VII/subchapter-C/part-740)

#### ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations) -- DDTC (State Department)

The ITAR (22 CFR 120-130) implements the Arms Export Control Act (AECA). It is administered by the **Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC)** in the Bureau of Political-Military Affairs at the U.S. Department of State.

**United States Munitions List (USML):**
- The USML (22 CFR 121.1) designates defense articles and services that are inherently military in character
- **21 Categories** covering items from firearms (Category I) to classified articles (Category XVI) to spacecraft (Category XV)
- Items on the USML require **DDTC authorization** for export, not BIS authorization
- The USML is periodically reviewed as required by Section 38 of the AECA

**Key 2024 Developments:**
- October 2024 proposed rule to revise Categories IV (Launch Vehicles/Missiles) and XV (Spacecraft)
- August 2024 **AUKUS exemption**: license-free trade for 70%+ of ITAR items between US, Australia, and UK (effective September 1, 2024)
- January 2025 final rule with targeted USML revisions (effective September 15, 2025)
- Category XXI phase-out in AES effective December 31, 2024

Sources: [DDTC Public Portal](https://www.pmddtc.state.gov/ddtc_public?id=ddtc_public_portal_itar_landing), [USML at eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-22/chapter-I/subchapter-M/part-121), [Federal Register USML Revisions](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/17/2025-01313/international-traffic-in-arms-regulations-us-munitions-list-targeted-revisions)

#### OFAC Sanctions and Embargoes

The **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)**, part of the U.S. Treasury Department, administers sanctions and embargo programs.

**Key Lists:**
- **SDN List (Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons)**: Approximately **12,000 names**. Assets are blocked and U.S. persons are generally prohibited from dealing with them
- **Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List**
- **Foreign Sanctions Evaders (FSE) List**
- **Non-SDN Menu-Based Sanctions (NS-MBS) List**

**Comprehensively Sanctioned Countries/Regions** (as of 2024): Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, and the Crimea/Donetsk/Luhansk regions of Ukraine.

**Compliance Requirement:** All parties to an export transaction must be screened against OFAC lists in addition to BIS lists. OFAC's **Sanctions List Search** tool is available at [sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov](https://sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov/).

Sources: [OFAC Programs & Country Information](https://ofac.treasury.gov/sanctions-programs-and-country-information), [SDN List](https://sanctionslist.ofac.treas.gov/Home/SdnList)

### 1.3 US Export Documentation

- **Commercial Invoice**: Describes goods, value, buyer/seller, terms of sale
- **Shipper's Letter of Instruction (SLI)**: Authorizes freight forwarder to act on behalf of the USPPI
- **Bill of Lading (ocean) / Air Waybill (air)**: Carrier-issued transport document; must contain the ITN (Internal Transaction Number) from AES or the NOEEI exemption citation
- **Certificate of Origin**: May be required by the destination country for FTA preferential tariff treatment or for general customs purposes
- **Export License** (when required): BIS license, DDTC license (DSP-5, TAA, etc.), or other agency authorization
- **Dangerous Goods Declaration**: Required under IATA (air) or IMDG Code (ocean) when shipping hazardous materials
- **Packing List**: Detailed list of goods, quantities, weights, dimensions

### 1.4 Routed Export Transactions

A **routed export transaction** occurs when the Foreign Principal Party in Interest (FPPI) -- the foreign buyer -- directs the movement of goods out of the U.S. and authorizes a U.S. agent to file the EEI.

**USPPI Responsibilities (even in routed transactions):**
- Must provide the FPPI's authorized agent with: USPPI name and address, EIN or DUNS, state of origin, FTZ status if applicable, domestic/foreign origin indicator, Schedule B or HTSUSA code, quantities/units of measure, and **ECCN or sufficient technical information to determine the ECCN**
- The USPPI remains **legally responsible for determining whether an export license is required** unless the FPPI signs a written statement accepting this responsibility
- Must retain documentation for 5 years

**FPPI's Authorized Agent Responsibilities:**
- Must obtain **power of attorney or written authorization** from the FPPI (not from the USPPI)
- Files the EEI in AES based on information obtained from the USPPI
- Provides the ITN or exemption code to the carrier
- Retains documentation for 5 years
- If requested, must provide the USPPI with a copy of the filed data elements and the power of attorney

**Critical Rule:** EEI cannot be shared with foreign entities or foreign governments. The filer must be **physically located in the United States**.

Sources: [15 CFR 30.3](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/15/30.3), [Census Bureau: Understanding Routed Exports](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/blogs/global-reach/2016/12/understanding_routed.html), [NCBFAA USPPI Responsibility Sheet](https://www.ncbfaa.org/docs/default-source/public-access-documents/usppi-export-responsibility-info-sheet-152722.pdf)

## 2. EU EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 2.1 Export Declaration

An electronic export declaration is required for all goods leaving the EU customs territory. The system has evolved from the **Export Control System (ECS)** to the **Automated Export System (AES)**, which is being rolled out across all EU member states.

**Three-Step Export Process:**

1. **Export Declaration Filed**: The declarant (exporter or customs representative) submits the electronic export declaration in the national customs system (e.g., DMS/AES in the Netherlands, PLDA/AES in Belgium, ATLAS/AES in Germany). Upon acceptance, customs assigns an **MRN (Movement Reference Number)** -- a unique identifier for the declaration. Customs performs risk analysis, may impose physical or documentary checks, and then grants **release for export**.

2. **Presentation at Office of Exit**: When goods arrive at the exit point (port, airport, border), the carrier submits an **exit notification (IE507)** referencing the MRN to the customs office of exit.

3. **Exit Confirmation**: After goods physically leave the EU customs territory, the carrier notifies the office of exit via message **IE590**. The system then issues a **Confirmation of Exit (IE599)** to the office of export. The IE599 is **critical proof** that goods have left the EU and is required for **VAT zero-rating**.

**MRN (Movement Reference Number):**
- Unique customs registration number assigned upon acceptance of the export declaration
- Primary reference linking all subsequent customs messages to the shipment
- The exporter must ensure the MRN is communicated to the carrier/transport operator who must present it at the office of exit
- Format: 18-character alphanumeric identifier

**AES Implementation Timeline:**
- Phase 1 (core functionalities): Implementation deadline **December 1, 2023**
- Phase 2 (harmonized interface with EMCS): Implementation deadline **February 13, 2024**
- Phase 3 (centralized clearance, simplified/supplementary declarations, NCTS interface): Transition window until **May 31, 2025**

**Simplified Export Procedures for AEO Holders:**

The EU **Authorised Economic Operator (AEO)** programme provides trusted traders with:
- **AEOC (Customs Simplifications)**: Faster document processing, reduced physical/documentary controls, priority treatment during inspections
- **AEOS (Security & Safety)**: Recognition of high security standards
- **AEOC & AEOS (Combined)**: Full benefits of both types
- **Centralised Clearance**: AEO holders may apply to lodge customs declarations at one customs office while presenting goods at another, even in a different EU member state
- **Mutual recognition**: AEO status granted by one EU country is recognized by all others, and via mutual recognition agreements with the US, Japan, China, and others

Sources: [EU AES](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/online-services/online-services-and-databases-customs/automated-export-system-aes_en), [Customs Support: What is MRN](https://www.customssupport.com/what-movement-reference-number-mrn-and-why-do-i-need-one/), [EU AEO Programme](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/customs/authorised-economic-operator/programme_en)

### 2.2 EU Export Controls

**EU Dual-Use Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2021/821):**
- Entered into force **September 8, 2021**, replacing Regulation (EC) No 428/2009
- Controls **exports, brokering, technical assistance, transit, and transfer** of dual-use items
- **Annex I** lists dual-use items requiring export authorization, aligned with: Wassenaar Arrangement, Missile Technology Control Regime (MTCR), Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG), and Australia Group
- **Annex IV** lists items that do not benefit from free movement within the EU internal market (require authorization even for intra-EU transfers)
- Introduces controls on **cybersurveillance technologies** that could be used to violate human rights
- Establishes a **Dual-Use Coordination Group** chaired by the Commission

**National Additions by Member States (2024):**
- More than half of EU member states have extended authorization requirements for **non-listed dual-use items** suspected of military end-use
- **France** (February 2024): Added national controls on quantum computer technologies and advanced electronic components
- **Germany** (July 2024): 21st Ordinance amending the Foreign Trade Ordinance added controls on parametric signal amplifiers, cryogenic cooling systems, and quantum components
- **11 member states** have imposed authorization requirements for intra-EU transfers of items not in Annex IV
- **5 member states** have imposed export authorization for cybersurveillance items linked to human rights violations

**2024 Annex I Update**: September 5, 2024, the Commission published a Delegated Regulation updating the dual-use control list.

**EU Common Military List**: Separate from the dual-use regulation, the EU maintains a Common Military List updated annually, covering military equipment that member states control under their national defense export control systems.

**Russia Restrictions**: Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/699 removed Russia from the scope of EU general export authorizations.

Sources: [EUR-Lex Dual-Use Summary](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/EN/legal-content/summary/dual-use-export-controls.html), [EU Member States Additional Measures](https://regandtrade.com/2024/10/eu-member-states-implement-additional-measures-to-regulate-export-of-non-listed-dual-use-items/), [BIS Presentation on EU Export Controls](https://www.bis.doc.gov/index.php/documents/compliance-training/3491-international-cooperation-and-export-controls-eu/file)

### 2.3 EU Export Documentation

- **Export Accompanying Document (EAD)**: Generated from the export declaration, accompanies the goods to the office of exit
- **Confirmation of Exit (IE599)**: Electronic confirmation that goods have left the EU. **Critical for VAT zero-rating** -- without it, the exporter may face VAT liability
- **Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Transport Document**: Standard trade documents
- **EUR.1 Movement Certificate**: Certificate of preferential origin for goods exported to countries with EU Free Trade Agreements. Issued by customs authorities upon exporter application with supplier's declaration proving origin. May be replaced by an **invoice declaration** for shipments under EUR 6,000 or by exporters with **approved exporter status**
- **Origin Declaration / REX System**: Under the Registered Exporter System, registered exporters can self-certify origin. Unregistered exporters may self-certify for consignments valued under EUR 6,000
- **End-User Certificates**: Required for controlled items, where the end user declares the intended use and commits to not re-export without authorization

**VAT Zero-Rating Proof Requirements:**
- Exporters must retain evidence that goods were transported outside the EU
- Evidence includes: export customs declaration (primary), transport documents (bill of lading, CMR), commercial invoice, import customs record at destination
- The **IE599 Confirmation of Exit** is the strongest proof
- Without adequate proof, the exporter has **no right to VAT zero-rating** and may owe VAT at the domestic rate

Sources: [EU VAT Exemptions](https://taxation-customs.ec.europa.eu/taxation/vat/vat-directive/vat-exemptions_en), [EUR.1 Certificates](https://www.chamber-international.com/exporting-chamber-international/documentation-for-export-and-import/eur-1-certificates/), [Alegrant: Proof of Export for VAT](https://alegrant.eu/proof-of-export-for-vat/)

## 3. CHINA EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 3.1 Export Declaration Process

**Single Window System:**
- China's Single Window platform has been operational since pilots began in 2012, with mandatory use phased in by 2024
- Enterprises submit declarations **once** to the online platform, which forwards them to relevant government agencies (GACC, MOFCOM, CIQ, MSA, etc.)
- Estimated to reduce total customs clearance costs by **50%**
- For low-risk compliant goods, the entire declaration-to-release process can be completed online
- Main export procedures reduced from **14 to 10** steps under recent reforms

**GACC (General Administration of Customs of China):**
- **GACC Announcement 277** (effective May 1, 2025): Updates the Regulations on Declaration Management of Import and Export Goods, covering electronic document submission, timelines, and amended certificate requirements
- Exporter registration numbers transitioned from 11-digit to **18-digit** format as of September 5, 2024
- Export inspection requirements (formerly under CIQ) are now consolidated under GACC

**Export License Requirements (MOFCOM administered):**
- **Three license types**: Single License (one export to one end user, valid 1 year), General License (for established compliant exporters), and Export Certificate (registration-based)
- Export prohibited and restricted goods are maintained in the **Catalogue of Technologies Prohibited and Restricted from Export** (currently **134 items**: 24 prohibited, 110 restricted)

Sources: [China Customs Clearance Process](http://english.customs.gov.cn/service/guide?c=cf4d843e-6f24-4aa2-ba98-f4f575c68fc9&k=51), [GACC Announcement 277](https://www.fas.usda.gov/data/china-gacc-announcement-277-regulation-declaration-management-import-and-export-goods), [China-Briefing on Export Controls](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/china-issues-new-export-control-regulations)

### 3.2 Export Tax Rebate System (VAT refund on exports)

The export tax rebate system refunds VAT and consumption tax paid during production and distribution of exported goods. Introduced in 1985, it is a cornerstone of China's trade competitiveness.

**Key Parameters:**
- Refund rates range from **0% to 13%**, depending on product classification
- Only businesses registered as **General VAT Taxpayers** can apply (subject to 13% output VAT rate)
- Application for a given year's rebate must be submitted by **April 30th** of the following year
- Processing time from submission to refund: **2-3 months**
- Formula: Refund = Export FOB value x Rebate rate (simplified)

**Current Rate Structure (post-December 1, 2024):**

| Product Category | Rebate Rate |
|---|---|
| Most manufactured goods | **13%** |
| Refined oil, photovoltaic products, batteries (reduced Dec 2024) | **9%** |
| Agricultural and certain other goods | **9%** |
| Some processed goods | **6%** |
| Aluminum, copper, chemically modified oils/fats (eliminated Dec 2024) | **0%** |

**Major November 2024 Policy Changes (Caishui [2024] No. 15, effective December 1, 2024):**
- Rebate rate reduced from 13% to 9% for **209 products** including refined oil, solar/photovoltaic products, and batteries
- Rebates **completely eliminated** for aluminum and copper products
- Port of Departure Tax Rebate Policy expanded (Caishui [2024] No. 31)

Sources: [China-Briefing: Export Tax Rebate Adjustments](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/navigating-chinas-latest-export-tax-rebate-adjustments-implications/), [GGI: VAT Refund Policies in China](https://www.ggi.com/news/indirect-taxes/vat-refund-policies-in-china/), [Acadia Advisory: Export Tax Rebates](https://acadiaadvisory.com/export-tax-rebates-in-china-eligibility-and-applicationprocess-for-exporters/)

### 3.3 China Export Controls

**Export Control Law** (effective December 1, 2020) with **Regulations on Export Control of Dual-Use Items** (effective December 1, 2024):

**Scope of Controlled Activities:**
- Physical transfer of dual-use items from PRC territory to overseas
- Provision of dual-use items by Chinese entities to foreign organizations/individuals (even **within China** -- this means technology transfers from Chinese employees to non-Chinese entities inside China may constitute "exporting")
- Transit, transshipment, transport, re-export from bonded areas to overseas

**Unified Dual-Use Items Export Control List:**
- Consolidates previously dispersed lists for nuclear, biological, chemical, and missile controls
- Organized into **10 industry sectors and 5 categories** with unified export control codes
- Approximately **700** controlled dual-use items total
- MOFCOM is developing an **ECCN-style classification system** modeled on the US Commerce Control List

**Key Restrictive Lists:**
1. **Control List (Controlled Parties List)**: Chinese version of the US Entity List. MOFCOM may list parties who violate end-user/end-use requirements, endanger national security, or use items for terrorist/WMD purposes
2. **Unreliable Entity List**: MOFCOM sanctions individuals/entities violating normal market principles; those sanctioned may be automatically incorporated into the Control List
3. **Watch List**: Similar to the US Unverified List. Parties who don't cooperate with end-user verification are placed here; exporters dealing with Watch List entities cannot use general licenses and must submit risk assessments
4. **Countermeasure List**: Based on the Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law

**Extraterritorial Reach:**
- The regulations cover products produced **outside the PRC** that contain, integrate, or are mixed with "specific" PRC-origin dual-use items
- Products produced outside the PRC **using** PRC-origin dual-use technologies are also covered

**Specific December 2024 Actions (Announcement No. 46):**
- Export of all dual-use items to **U.S. military users or military end uses is prohibited**
- License applications for exports to the US of items related to **gallium, germanium, antimony, and superhard materials** will not be approved as a matter of principle

**Temporary Controls**: Items not on the control list can be controlled temporarily for up to **2 years**, with two extensions possible.

Sources: [Covington: China Dual-Use Regulations](https://www.cov.com/en/news-and-insights/insights/2024/10/china-promulgates-new-dual-use-export-control-regulations), [Morrison Foerster: China's New Framework](https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/241216-china-s-new-export-control-framework-key-changes), [Orrick: China Dual-Use Items](https://www.orrick.com/en/Insights/2024/11/China-Issues-Regulation-on-Export-Control-of-Dual-Use-Items)

## 4. BRAZIL EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 4.1 Siscomex Exportacao and the DU-E

**Portal Unico (Single Foreign Trade Portal):**
- Launched in 2014 to reduce bureaucracy, time, and costs in Brazilian foreign trade
- All international trade must be registered with **Siscomex** (Sistema Integrado de Comercio Exterior), established by Decreto No. 660 of September 25, 1992
- Access requires **digital certificate** issued under ICP-Brasil (Brazilian Public Key Infrastructure)
- Registration is also known as **RADAR** (Registro e Rastreamento da Atuacao dos Intervenientes Aduaneiros)

**DU-E (Declaracao Unica de Exportacao -- Single Export Declaration):**
- Electronic document containing all information characterizing a goods export operation
- **Replaced** the old two-step process of RE (Registro de Exportacao) + DDE (Declaracao de Despacho de Exportacao)
- Integrates with the **Nota Fiscal Eletronica (NF-e)** for automatic data feeding and reduced errors
- Incorporates a **Unique Consignment Reference (UCR)** meeting WCO recommendations
- Allows **simultaneous** customs and non-customs controls
- Already fully operational on the export side; served as the model for the import-side DUIMP rollout in 2024-2025

**Export Licensing:**
- **Automatic licensing**: Granted for most exports; processed within approximately 10 business days
- **Non-automatic licensing**: Required for products posing health, environmental, or security risks; processing up to 60 days
- Applications submitted through Siscomex to **DECEX** (Department of Foreign Trade Operations), a unit of **SECEX** (Foreign Trade Secretariat)
- **MCTI** (Ministry of Science, Technology, and Innovation) is responsible for dual-use goods exports
- **Ministry of Defense** regulates trade in munitions items
- Brazil participates in MTCR, NSG, and controls CWC-scheduled chemicals

Sources: [WCO News: Brazil's Unique Export Declaration](https://mag.wcoomd.org/magazine/wco-news-87/brazils-unique-export-declaration-modernization-inspired-by-international-standards/), [Wilson Sons: DU-E](https://wilsonsons.com.br/en/blog/single-export-declaration/), [BIS: Brazil Export Control Information](https://www.bis.doc.gov/index.php/licensing/220-eco-country-pages/1051-brazil-export-control-information)

### 4.2 Export Tax Benefits

**Drawback Regime (three modalities):**

1. **Suspension Drawback**: Suspends payment of Import Duty (II), IPI, PIS, COFINS, PIS-Import, and COFINS-Import on goods (imported or domestically acquired) used in manufacturing products for export. ICMS exemption applies to imports only. As of January 2023 (Law 14440/2022), extends to **services** directly linked to export (intermediation, cargo insurance, customs clearance, storage, transportation, freight forwarding)

2. **Exemption Drawback**: Exempts II and reduces IPI, PIS, COFINS, PIS/PASEP-Import, and COFINS-Import to zero on imports of goods **equivalent** to those already used in exported products. No ICMS benefit

3. **Refund Drawback**: Total or partial refund of taxes paid on imported inputs used in manufacturing exported goods

**IPI (Federal Excise Tax):**
- Rate generally 0-15%
- Brazilian exports are **exempt** from IPI
- Under tax reform, IPI rates will be reduced to zero in 2027 (except for Manaus Free Trade Zone products)

**PIS and COFINS (Social Contributions):**
- Combined rate of **9.25%** under non-cumulative regime (COFINS 7.6% + PIS 1.65%)
- PIS and COFINS are **not levied** on export transactions

**ICMS (State VAT):**
- Rates typically **17-19%** depending on state
- Exports are **exempt** from ICMS under **Lei Kandir** (Complementary Law 87/1996), which exempted exports of primary and semi-manufactured goods
- Under tax reform, ICMS will be replaced by **IBS** (Tax on Goods and Services), fully effective in 2033

**Upcoming Tax Reform:**
- CBS (Contribution on Goods and Services) replaces PIS and COFINS, fully effective 2027
- IBS (Tax on Goods and Services) replaces ICMS and ISS, fully effective 2033
- Transition period begins 2026

Sources: [Trade.gov: Brazil Import Tariffs](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-import-tariffs), [International Tax Review: Brazil Drawback](https://www.internationaltaxreview.com/article/2atv32mg832llrg3lju2o/sponsored/brazilian-government-boosts-the-benefits-applicable-under-the-drawback-regime), [PWC: Brazil Corporate Other Taxes](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/brazil/corporate/other-taxes)

### 4.3 Export Documentation

- **Nota Fiscal (NF-e)**: Electronic tax invoice -- **mandatory** for all goods movements in Brazil, including exports. Integrates with the DU-E
- **Commercial Invoice**: International commercial document describing goods, value, parties
- **Packing List**: Detailed contents of the shipment
- **Bill of Lading / Air Waybill**: Carrier-issued transport document
- **Certificate of Origin**: For FTA preferential tariff treatment at the destination (e.g., MERCOSUR, ALADI agreements)
- **DU-E**: The export declaration itself, processed through Siscomex

## 5. INDIA EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 5.1 Shipping Bill

The Shipping Bill is the official customs declaration for exports from India, filed electronically through **ICEGATE** (Indian Customs EDI Gateway).

**Types of Shipping Bills:**

| Type | Paper Color | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Free (Duty-Free)** | White | Goods not subject to export duty and not eligible for duty drawback |
| **Dutiable** | Yellow | Goods subject to export duty |
| **Drawback** | Green (Yellow after drawback paid) | Goods eligible for refund of customs duties on imported inputs |
| **Ex-Bond** | Pink | Goods imported and stored in bonded warehouses being re-exported |
| **Coastal** | -- | Goods shipped between domestic ports (not an export document) |
| **DEPB Scheme** | Blue | Goods under the Duty Entitlement Passbook Scheme |

**Filing Process:**
1. **Registration**: Exporter registers on ICEGATE with IEC (Import Export Code), CHA license number, and AD Code of bank
2. **Submission**: Fill in details and upload documents through ICEGATE portal; digital signatures required for secure filing
3. **Assessment**: Assistant Commissioner (Exports) assesses the shipping bill if: shipment value exceeds INR 10 lakh, free samples exceed INR 20,000, or drawback claim exceeds INR 1 lakh
4. **Examination**: Customs examines goods and documents
5. **Let Export Order (LEO)**: Upon satisfaction, customs issues the LEO -- the **final legal approval** for export. Without LEO, goods cannot be loaded onto vessels or aircraft
6. **Final Shipping Bill**: Printed after LEO is granted

**Key Information in a Shipping Bill:**
- Exporter, buyer/importer, customs agent details
- Vessel name, port of loading and discharge
- Cargo particulars (gross/net weight, nature of goods)
- Invoice details (number, nature of payment, value in both currencies)
- GST and export duty information
- FOB price and insurance amount
- Container numbers
- Duty drawback details (if applicable)

Sources: [ICEGATE Portal](https://www.icegate.gov.in), [MyGSTRefund: Shipping Bill Types](https://www.mygstrefund.com/blog/shipping-bill-types-formats), [IndiaFilings: Shipping Bill Guide](https://www.indiafilings.com/learn/shipping-bill/)

### 5.2 Export Incentive Schemes

**RoDTEP (Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products):**
- Replaced the WTO-non-compliant **MEIS** (Merchandise Exports from India Scheme) in 2021
- Reimburses **embedded central, state, and local taxes** not compensated under other schemes (electricity duty, mandi tax, coal cess, toll tax, stamp duty on import/export documentation, fuel levies)
- Benefit: duty credit/electronic scrips (e-scrips) as a percentage of FOB export value
- **Revised rates (October 10, 2024)**: 0.3% to 3.9% of export value (down from 0.5% to 4.3%)
- Extended for **DTA units** until September 30, 2025
- AA/EOU/SEZ eligibility expired February 5, 2025
- **Annual RoDTEP Return (ARR)** mandatory from FY 2023-24 for claims exceeding INR 10 million, filed through DGFT portal by March 31

**Advance Authorization (AA):**
- Duty-free import of inputs physically incorporated in export products
- Covers raw materials, fuel, packaging, oil, catalyst consumed in production
- Exempts: Basic Customs Duty, IGST, and cess
- Quantity based on **Standard Input-Output Norms (SION)** defined by DGFT
- **Minimum value addition**: 15% within 18 months

**DFIA (Duty Free Import Authorization):**
- Similar to AA but transferable after export obligation is fulfilled
- Separate DFIA issued for each SION
- **Minimum value addition**: 20%
- Exports must be completed within 12 months of online filing

**EPCG (Export Promotion Capital Goods):**
- Allows **duty-free import of capital goods** (machinery, technology) for manufacturing
- Exemption from Basic Customs Duty and IGST
- **Export obligation**: 6 times the duty saved within **6 years**
- Authorization valid for import for **24 months** (no revalidation)
- Compatible with RoDTEP (can be claimed simultaneously)

**Duty Drawback:**
- Refund of customs duty paid on imported materials used in manufacturing exported goods
- Claimed through the drawback shipping bill type

Sources: [DGFT RoDTEP](https://www.dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=RoDTEP), [India-Briefing: RoDTEP Compliance](https://www.india-briefing.com/news/indias-rodtep-scheme-for-exporters-key-extensions-and-compliance-updates-35325.html/), [RSM India: Export Incentives](https://www.rsm.global/india/insights/tax-insights/export-incentives-in-india-for-exporters)

### 5.3 Export Controls

**DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade)** administers India's export controls under the Ministry of Commerce & Industry.

**SCOMET List (Special Chemicals, Organisms, Materials, Equipment and Technologies):**
- India's export control list for dual-use items, nuclear-related items, military goods, and associated software/technology
- Maintained under **Appendix 3 of Schedule 2** of the ITC (HS) Classification
- Organized into **8 categories**
- Aligned with the **MTCR and Wassenaar Arrangement**
- Export permitted **only** with export authorization (unless prohibited or subject to conditions)

**2024 Updates:**
- Updated SCOMET list incorporating changes from multilateral export control regimes
- **Department of Defence Production (DDP)** designated as licensing authority for **Category 6** (military end-use) items
- DGFT enhancing its e-platform for licensing
- General Authorization policy liberalized for drones, dual-use chemicals, and telecom equipment
- Policies added for Repairs in India, Repeat Orders, and Intra Company Transfers (GAICT)

**Licensing Process:**
- Application filed online via DGFT portal using form **ANF 2O** (for SCOMET items) or **ANF 2B** (for non-SCOMET restricted items)
- Processing time: **3 to 12 weeks** (longer for SCOMET items requiring inter-ministerial consultation)
- License validity: typically **24 months** for SCOMET items
- **Technology transfers** (including electronic, cloud, or verbal) of SCOMET-related technologies require special authorization

**Compliance:**
- Companies regularly exporting SCOMET items should maintain an **Internal Compliance Program (ICP)**
- Exporting restricted items without a license: penalties up to **5 times the value** of the goods, prosecution under FTDR Act, potential imprisonment, IEC suspension/cancellation, placement on Denied Entity List

Sources: [MEA: India Strategic Trade Controls](https://www.mea.gov.in/India-Strategic-Trade-Controls-and-SCOMET-List.htm), [DGFT Export Management System](https://www.dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=export-management-system), [DevDiscourse: SCOMET 2024 Amendments](https://www.devdiscourse.com/article/business/3073408-dg-of-foreign-trade-updates-export-control-list-with-2024-scomet-amendments)

## 6. CROSS-CUTTING EXPORT TOPICS

### 6.1 Export vs Import: Key Structural Differences for Software

| Dimension | Export Clearance | Import Clearance |
|---|---|---|
| **Classification** | At origin (Schedule B, CN code, etc.) | At destination (HTSUS, CN code, etc.) |
| **Governing Rules** | Origin country's export control laws | Destination country's import/tariff laws |
| **Controls** | Export controls (EAR, ITAR, EU Dual-Use, etc.) | Import duties, quotas, standards, sanctions |
| **Primary Responsible Party** | Exporter / shipper | Importer / consignee |
| **Government Revenue** | Rarely generates revenue (exceptions: export duties in some countries) | Primary revenue source (customs duties, VAT/GST) |
| **Key Compliance Risk** | Sending controlled items to wrong destinations/parties | Undervaluation, misclassification, prohibited goods |
| **Filing System** | AES (US), AES/ECS (EU), Single Window (China), DU-E (Brazil), ICEGATE (India) | ACE (US), ICS (EU), Single Window (China), DUIMP (Brazil), ICEGATE (India) |

**A single international shipment requires BOTH:**
- Export clearance at the **origin country** (filed by exporter/exporter's agent)
- Import clearance at the **destination country** (filed by importer/importer's agent)

These are **independent** processes governed by **different** legal frameworks. Software must model them as linked but separate workflows.

**Incoterms Impact on Responsibility:**

| Incoterm | Export Clearance | Import Clearance |
|---|---|---|
| **EXW** | Buyer (in practice, often seller helps) | Buyer |
| **FCA / FAS / FOB** | Seller | Buyer |
| **CFR / CIF / CPT / CIP** | Seller | Buyer |
| **DAP / DPU** | Seller | Buyer |
| **DDP** | **Seller** | **Seller** |

Under **DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)**, the seller handles **both** export and import clearance, pays all duties and taxes, and delivers goods ready for unloading at the buyer's destination. This is the **only** Incoterm placing import clearance responsibility on the seller. DDP requires the seller to comply with legislation in countries where it may have no presence, potentially requiring registration as importer and for VAT/GST purposes.

**Warning for DDP:** In some countries, foreign entities are **prohibited** from carrying out import customs formalities. In such cases, parties should use **DAP** instead.

Sources: [GEODIS: Incoterms in Customs Clearance](https://geodis.com/us-en/blog/goods-transportation-integrated-logistics-solutions/role-incoterms-customs-clearance), [ICC Academy: EXW or DDP](https://academy.iccwbo.org/incoterms/article/incoterms-2020-exw-or-ddp/), [Trade Finance Global: DDP Guide](https://www.tradefinanceglobal.com/incoterms/ddp-delivery-duty-paid/)

### 6.2 Export Status Lifecycle (for Software Tracking)

Based on research across the various country systems, a clearance intelligence platform should track these **export-side statuses**:

**Pre-Filing:**
1. **DRAFT** -- Export declaration being prepared, data collection from commercial documents
2. **PENDING_CLASSIFICATION** -- Goods being classified (Schedule B, CN code, SCOMET check, etc.)
3. **PENDING_LICENSE_DETERMINATION** -- Export control screening underway (ECCN determination, denied party screening, ITAR jurisdiction check)
4. **LICENSE_REQUIRED** -- Export license required; application to be submitted
5. **LICENSE_APPLICATION_SUBMITTED** -- License application filed with relevant authority (BIS SNAP-R, DDTC, MOFCOM, DGFT, etc.)
6. **LICENSE_GRANTED** -- Export license approved
7. **LICENSE_DENIED** -- Export license denied

**Filing & Clearance:**
8. **FILED** -- Export declaration submitted electronically (EEI in AES, export declaration in EU AES, DU-E in Siscomex, Shipping Bill in ICEGATE, declaration in China Single Window)
9. **ACCEPTED** -- Declaration accepted by customs; reference number assigned (ITN in US, MRN in EU, DU-E number in Brazil, Shipping Bill number in India)
10. **UNDER_REVIEW** -- Customs performing risk analysis or document review
11. **EXAMINATION_ORDERED** -- Physical or documentary examination ordered by customs
12. **EXAMINATION_COMPLETE** -- Examination finished
13. **RELEASED** / **CLEARED** -- Customs grants release/clearance for export (LEO in India, release in US/EU/China/Brazil)
14. **HOLD** -- Customs has placed a hold (possible violation, missing documents, denied party match)
15. **REJECTED** -- Declaration rejected by customs (errors, violations)

**Post-Clearance / Departure:**
16. **GOODS_AT_EXIT** -- Goods presented at port/airport/border for departure (EU: IE507 exit notification)
17. **LOADED** -- Goods loaded on carrier (vessel, aircraft, truck, rail)
18. **DEPARTED** -- Carrier has departed with goods
19. **EXIT_CONFIRMED** -- Customs confirms goods have left the country (EU: IE599 Confirmation of Exit -- critical for VAT zero-rating)
20. **COMPLETED** -- Export entry finalized; all confirmations received

**Exception Statuses:**
21. **AMENDMENT_REQUIRED** -- Declaration needs correction (15 CFR 30.9 in US; amendment processes in other countries)
22. **CANCELLED** -- Export declaration cancelled (goods not exported)
23. **POST_DEPARTURE_FILED** -- EEI filed after departure under approved postdeparture filing program (US only, within 5 calendar days)

**How Export Clearance Interacts with Carrier Pickup and Departure:**
- In most countries, customs **release/clearance must occur BEFORE** goods can be loaded onto the carrier
- The carrier cannot accept goods without proof of export clearance (ITN/exemption citation in US, MRN in EU, etc.)
- In the EU, the carrier must submit the exit notification at the border and confirm departure; without the IE599 confirmation, the exporter faces VAT risk
- In the US, the ITN from the AES filing must appear on the bill of lading or air waybill before export
- In India, the LEO must be granted before goods can be loaded; after loading, the customs officer issues a "Let Ship" order
- In Brazil, the DU-E must be processed and the goods cleared before they can leave the customs zone

Sources: [LCS Logistics: After Export Clearance](https://lcslogistics.com/after-export-customs-clearance-completes/), [Artemus: Customs Release Status](https://artemusgroupusa.com/shipment-has-been-given-a-release-status-by-customs/), [TrackingMore: Customs Statuses](https://m.trackingmore.com/tracking_status_sort-en-3.html)
