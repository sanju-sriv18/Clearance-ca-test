## 1. CHINA CUSTOMS (GACC - General Administration of Customs of China / 中华人民共和国海关总署)

### 1.1 Organization Structure

**National Level:**
The General Administration of Customs of China (GACC / 海关总署) is a ministry-level agency directly under the State Council. It is the supreme customs authority responsible for managing the import and export of goods and services nationwide. As of 2025, GACC employs approximately 100,000 staff throughout the country.

Current leadership: Sun Meijun serves as Minister of GACC (as of August 2025), with Vice Minister Wang Lingjun handling operational execution.

**Key GACC Functions:**
- Customs supervision, regulation, and enforcement
- Collection of import/export duties and taxes
- Entry-exit health quarantine (assumed from former CIQ/AQSIQ in 2018)
- Inspection and quarantine of animals, plants, and their products
- Imported food and cosmetics safety inspection
- Trade statistics compilation
- Anti-smuggling enforcement
- Intellectual property rights protection at the border
- Formulating and managing bonded trade supervision
- Developing the import/export commodity catalogue
- Negotiating rules of origin under bilateral/multilateral agreements
- Implementing anti-dumping, countervailing, and safeguard tariff measures

**Local Level:**
GACC oversees **42 customs districts** (直属海关) which operate through a total of **678 customs houses** (隶属海关) nationwide. A regional office in Guangzhou coordinates the Guangdong area. Each customs district manages the ports and border crossings within its jurisdiction.

**Key Internal Departments** include the Administration Office, Discipline Inspection Office, Tariff Department, Supervision Department, Statistics and Analysis Department, Anti-Smuggling Bureau, and the former CIQ-related departments (Food Safety, Animal and Plant Quarantine, Health Quarantine).

### 1.2 International Trade Single Window (国际贸易"单一窗口")

China operates the **China International Trade Single Window** (中国国际贸易单一窗口), an electronic platform that consolidates import/export declarations, inspection/quarantine applications, tax payments, and regulatory filings into a single portal. Key features:
- Enterprises submit import/export customs declaration forms through the "Goods Declaration" section
- Supports both "Summary Declaration" and "Full Declaration" modes under the two-step declaration system
- Integrates with the Electronic Port IC Card system for identity verification and electronic signatures
- Overseas food manufacturers register through GACC's Single Window/CIFER system
- Links with MOFCOM licensing, SAFE foreign exchange, and tax authority systems

### 1.3 Classification System

China uses the **China Customs Commodity HS Code (CCCHS)** system, which extends the international Harmonized System:

| Digits | Function |
|--------|----------|
| 1-6 | International HS code (harmonized globally under WCO) |
| 7-8 | National subheadings (中国子目) for tariff, statistics, and trade policy |
| 9-10 | Supervisory/regulatory codes identifying licensing and regulatory conditions |
| 11-13 | CIQ additional codes (检验检疫附加编码) added after the 2018 CIQ merger |

As of 2026, China's tariff schedule covers **8,972 product categories** (8-digit tariff lines), up from 8,960 in 2025. The legal responsibility for correct classification lies with the importer or exporter. Misclassification can result in customs delays, fines, or confiscation.

### 1.4 Legal Framework

**Primary Laws:**
- **Customs Law of the People's Republic of China** (中华人民共和国海关法) - the foundational legislation
- **Foreign Trade Law** (对外贸易法) - governs import/export licensing, trade remedies
- **Export Control Law** (出口管制法) - effective December 1, 2020, comprehensive dual-use/export controls
- **Tariff Regulations of the PRC** (关税条例) - governs tariff rates, classification, valuation

**Key Implementing Regulations:**
- Regulations on the Administration of Import and Export Duties
- Regulations on Customs Valuation of Imported and Exported Goods
- Administrative Regulations on Customs Declaration (GACC Order No. 277, effective May 1, 2025) - replaces the 2003 version
- GACC Decree No. 248/249 on overseas food manufacturer registration and food safety
- Regulations on the Registration and Administration of Overseas Manufacturers of Imported Food (GACC Order No. 280, effective June 1, 2026)

## 2. IMPORT/EXPORT PROCESS

### 2.1 Pre-Registration Requirements

Before engaging in import/export trade in China, entities must complete multiple registrations:

1. **MOFCOM Foreign Trade Registration** (商务部对外贸易经营者备案): Required for all entities engaging in foreign trade. The company must have "import/export" (进出口) included in its business scope on its business license.

2. **Customs Registration Certificate** (海关注册登记证书): Issued by local customs upon registration. Companies receive a unique **Customs Registration Code** (海关注册编码), typically a 10-digit code serving as the sole identifier for customs declarations, duty exemptions, foreign exchange settlements, and inspections.

3. **Electronic Port IC Card** (电子口岸IC卡): Required for data sharing, electronic signatures, and identity verification on the Electronic Port Platform.

4. **SAFE Registration** (外汇管理局备案): Registration with the State Administration of Foreign Exchange for cross-border payments.

5. **Tax Registration**: Complete VAT and other tax registrations, particularly for export VAT rebate eligibility.

**Entity Requirements:**
- Must be a registered Chinese entity (WFOE, JV, etc.) with valid business license
- Must have opened a basic RMB bank account
- Must hold a "Registered Import and Export Enterprise Code" tied to their business license number

**Credit Rating System:** GACC classifies enterprises into credit ratings: Advanced Certified Enterprise (高级认证企业), General Certified Enterprise (一般认证企业), General Credit Enterprise (一般信用企业), and Dishonest Enterprise (失信企业). These ratings affect clearance speed and supervision intensity.

### 2.2 Import Declaration Process

China offers three main declaration modes:

#### (a) Advance Declaration (提前申报)

Importers may submit customs declarations **before goods arrive** by providing the description, specifications, and quantity of imported goods. Upon arrival, customs examines the goods and releases them immediately since the necessary information was already reviewed. This significantly speeds up clearance.

#### (b) Standard Declaration (一般申报)

The traditional single-step declaration at the time of goods arrival. All required information and documents are submitted simultaneously.

#### (c) Two-Step Declaration (两步申报)

Introduced in pilot form in October 2019, expanded nationally from January 1, 2020, and significantly reformed in 2025:

**Step 1 - Summary Declaration (概要申报):**
- Submit minimum required declaration items
- Goods can be released after this stage
- No need to select complex items related to taxes, certificates, or inspections during this stage
- Supporting documents (certificates of origin, inspection certificates) can optionally be uploaded

**Step 2 - Complete Declaration (完整申报):**
- Must be completed within **14 days** from the date the inbound transport means is declared
- Late submission triggers late declaration fees (滞报金) per GACC Order No. 277
- Enterprises may apply for reduction or exemption of late fees for customs-related delays

**2025 Reforms (GACC Announcements No. 44 and No. 115):**
- Phased rollout: Pilot from May 6, 2025; full national implementation from June 16, 2025
- Integrated the "two-step declaration" and "single declaration, phased processing" interfaces
- Streamlined declaration items at the summary stage
- Enterprise eligibility: domestic consignee must not be rated as a "dishonest enterprise"
- Tax guarantees required for taxable goods before summary declaration

### 2.3 Required Documents

Standard import declaration documents:
- **Customs Declaration Form** (报关单)
- **Commercial Invoice** (商业发票)
- **Packing List** (装箱单)
- **Bill of Lading / Airway Bill** (提单/空运运单)
- **Certificate of Origin** (原产地证书) - for preferential tariff claims
- **Insurance Certificate** (保险单)
- **Sales Contract** (买卖合同)
- **Import License** (进口许可证) - for restricted goods
- **Inspection and Quarantine Certificates** (检验检疫证书) - where required
- **CCC Certificate** (中国强制性产品认证证书) - for applicable products
- **Automatic Import License** (自动进口许可证) - for certain monitored goods
- **Quarantine Approval** (检疫审批) - for animals, plants, and related products

### 2.4 CIQ Integration (2018 Merger)

In March 2018, the former China Inspection and Quarantine Bureau (CIQ / 中国出入境检验检疫局), which had operated under AQSIQ (国家质量监督检验检疫总局), was merged into GACC. Key impacts:

- All CIQ offices at Chinese ports were integrated into GACC's structure
- GACC now directly handles: entry-exit health quarantine, animal/plant inspection and quarantine, food and cosmetics safety inspection, and commodity quality inspection
- HS codes were expanded from 10 digits to **13 digits** to incorporate CIQ codes (digits 11-13)
- Reduced bureaucratic burden with unified single-window filing
- Standardized inspection requirements across regions
- Stricter enforcement with consolidated authority

**Food Import Conformity Assessment** includes: assessment of foreign country food safety systems, overseas production enterprise registration, importer/exporter record filing, quarantine approval, inspection of conformity certificates, documentation verification, on-site inspection, supervisory sampling, and import/sales record inspection.

## 3. TARIFF STRUCTURE

### 3.1 Rate Categories

China's import tariff system has **six rate categories** (关税税率种类):

| Rate Type | Chinese Term | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **MFN Rate** (最惠国税率) | Applied to WTO members; China's default rate for most trading partners |
| **Conventional/Agreement Rate** (协定税率) | Applied under bilateral/multilateral FTAs; lower than MFN |
| **Preferential Rate** (特惠税率) | Applied to least-developed countries under special arrangements |
| **General Rate** (普通税率) | Applied to non-MFN countries; very high rates (often 2-3x MFN) |
| **Tariff Rate Quota (TRQ) Rate** (关税配额税率) | Applied to goods within quota (8 categories: wheat, corn, rice, sugar, wool, wool top, cotton, fertilizer) |
| **Provisional/Temporary Rate** (暂定税率) | Seasonal/temporary adjustments; typically lower than MFN |

### 3.2 MFN Rates (最惠国税率)

Applied to imports from WTO member countries. Rates vary widely by HS code, typically ranging from **0% to 65%**, with most manufactured goods falling in the 0-35% range. The weighted average MFN tariff rate is approximately 7.3%.

### 3.3 Provisional/Temporary Rates (暂定税率)

- As of 2025 and 2026, **935 product categories** are subject to provisional import tariff rates, lower than MFN rates
- Designed to support domestic development, reduce costs for key inputs, and promote new industries
- Examples in 2026: recycled black powder for lithium-ion batteries reduced from 6.5% to 3%; unroasted pyrite reduced from 1% to 0%
- Some rates raised for items where domestic production capacity has grown (e.g., micro motors, printing machines, sulfuric acid)
- New tariff lines added in 2026: intelligent biomimetic robots, bio-aviation kerosene, under-forest ginseng

### 3.4 Import VAT (进口增值税)

VAT is assessed on the composite value of (CIF value + customs duty):

| VAT Rate | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **13%** | Standard rate - most imported goods |
| **9%** | Reduced rate - agricultural products, utilities, books, certain essential goods |

**Formula:** Import VAT = (CIF Value + Customs Duty + Consumption Tax) x VAT Rate

VAT is recoverable as input credit for registered businesses. Export VAT rebates (出口退税) are available at various rates.

Note: A new consolidated **VAT Law** takes effect January 1, 2026, replacing earlier regulations while maintaining the core framework.

### 3.5 Consumption Tax (消费税)

Applied to specific luxury goods, health-harmful products, and high-energy-consumption items. Calculated using ad valorem, quantity-based, or compound methods. Rate range: **1% to 56%**.

**Detailed Rates:**

**Tobacco (烟草):**

| Category A cigarettes (producer price >= 7 RMB/pack) | 56% + RMB 0.003/stick |

| Wholesale level surcharge | 11% + RMB 0.005/stick |
| Cigars | 36% |
| Cut tobacco | 30% |

**Alcohol (酒类):**
| Category | Rate |
|----------|------|

**Other Categories:**

**Surcharges on top of VAT/Consumption Tax:**
- Urban Maintenance and Construction Tax (城市维护建设税): 7% (city) / 5% (county) / 1% (other)
- Education Surcharge (教育费附加): 3%
- Local Education Surcharge (地方教育附加): 2%

### 3.6 Cross-Border E-Commerce (CBEC) Tax Policy

China maintains a separate, preferential tax regime for cross-border e-commerce retail imports (跨境电子商务零售进口):

**Positive List (正面清单):** Covers **1,476 commodity categories** including cosmetics, dairy, beverages, infant formula, health supplements, etc. Only products on this list qualify for CBEC treatment.

**Tax Treatment:**
- Per-transaction limit: **RMB 5,000**
- Annual per-person limit: **RMB 26,000**
- Within these limits: import tariff rate is temporarily set at **0%**; VAT and consumption tax are levied at **70%** of statutory rates
- Common effective rate for standard goods: approximately **9.1%**
- Transactions exceeding RMB 5,000 (for indivisible goods) but within the annual limit: full tax applies
- Excess beyond annual limit: treated as general trade

**Formula:** CBEC Tax = [(Consumption Tax Rate + VAT Rate) / (1 - Consumption Tax Rate)] x 70%

**CBEC Pilot Zones:** As of late 2024, **165 comprehensive CBEC zones** (跨境电子商务综合试验区) have been approved by the State Council nationwide.

**Key Advantage:** Products imported via CBEC channels can bypass certain regulatory requirements (e.g., CCC certification, NMPA registration for cosmetics), making it a fast-track market entry route for foreign brands.

### 3.7 Total Import Cost Formula

**Total Import Cost = CIF Value + Customs Duty + Import VAT + Consumption Tax (if applicable)**

Where:
- Customs Duty = CIF Value x Applicable Tariff Rate
- Consumption Tax = (CIF Value + Customs Duty) / (1 - Consumption Tax Rate) x Consumption Tax Rate (for ad valorem)
- Import VAT = (CIF Value + Customs Duty + Consumption Tax) x VAT Rate

### 4.2 Rules of Origin (原产地规则)

Rules of origin determine whether products qualify for preferential tariff treatment under FTAs:

**General Approach:** Products with a minimum of **40% regional value content** generally qualify for preferential tariffs under most Chinese FTAs.

**Methods of Determination:**
- **Wholly obtained criterion** (完全获得标准): for goods entirely grown/produced in one country
- **Substantial transformation criterion** (实质性改变标准): for goods with multi-country inputs, using:
  - Change in tariff classification (税则归类改变)
  - Regional value content threshold (区域价值成分)
  - Specific processing requirements (特定加工工序)
  - Combination rules

**Certificate of Origin Types for China:**
| Form | Agreement |
|------|-----------|
| Form E | China-ASEAN FTA |
| Form F | China-Chile FTA |
| Form N | China-New Zealand FTA |
| Form P | China-Pakistan FTA |
| Form B | APTA/Bangkok Agreement |
| Form R | RCEP |
| Form L | China-Peru FTA |
| Form A | GSP (Generalized System of Preferences) |

**Issuing Authorities:** China Council for the Promotion of International Trade (CCPIT / 中国国际贸易促进委员会) or local GACC branches. Under RCEP, approved exporters may issue self-declarations of origin.

## 5. CUSTOMS VALUATION IN CHINA

### 5.1 Valuation Basis: CIF

China values imports on a **CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight)** basis, meaning the dutiable value includes:
- Transaction price of goods
- Packing costs
- International freight charges
- Insurance premiums
- Seller's commission

This differs from countries like the US (which uses FOB). China's approach is consistent with WTO Customs Valuation Agreement (GATT Article VII).

### 5.2 Transaction Value Method (成交价格法)

The **primary method** for determining customs value. The transaction value is the price actually paid or payable for goods when sold for export to China, subject to certain adjustments.

### 5.3 Additions to Transaction Value

Per GACC regulations, the following must be added to the declared transaction value:

- **Commissions and brokerage fees** (not buying commissions)
- **Cost of containers** treated as one with the goods
- **Packing costs** (labor and materials)
- **Materials, components, tools supplied by the buyer** (buyer-provided materials / 买方提供的物料)
- **Royalties and licensing fees** (特许权使用费) related to the imported goods and paid as a condition of sale
- **Resale proceeds** accruing to the seller
- **Transportation and insurance costs** to the port of importation (for CIF)
- **Special packaging design fees**
- **Installation and commissioning fees** (overseas portion under CIF)

### 5.4 Royalties and Licensing Fees (特许权使用费)

Per Article 13 of the GACC Customs Valuation Order, royalties/license fees must be added to transaction value if they are:
1. **Related to the imported goods** (与进口货物有关)
2. **A condition of the sale** (作为进口货物销售的条件)

This is a major compliance area for multinational enterprises. Key considerations:
- Trademark fees, technology licensing fees, and other intangible payments must be analyzed
- GACC has implemented an **"Intelligent Pricing Review System"** (智能审价系统) as of 2025
- Importers must declare whether royalties/license fees have been paid
- False declarations can lead to administrative penalties

### 5.5 Related-Party Transactions (关联交易)

Special treatment applies to transactions between related parties:
- Transaction value is accepted only if the relationship **did not influence the price**
- GACC may conduct further inquiry if it has reason to doubt the declared price
- Since March 2016, declaration forms require disclosure of related-party relationships and confirmation that prices were not influenced
- Increasing enforcement and coordination between customs valuation and transfer pricing
- Penalties possible for untruthful disclosure or lack of robust evidence
- Companies must ensure consistency between transfer pricing policies and customs valuation positions

### 5.6 Alternative Valuation Methods

When transaction value cannot be used (or is rejected), GACC applies methods in sequence:
1. Transaction value of identical goods (相同货物成交价格法)
2. Transaction value of similar goods (类似货物成交价格法)
3. Deductive value method (倒扣价格法)
4. Computed value method (计算价格法)
5. Reasonable means / fallback method (合理方法)

## 6. SPECIAL CUSTOMS ZONES

### 6.1 Pilot Free Trade Zones (自贸试验区)

China has established **22 pilot Free Trade Zones (FTZs)** plus the Hainan Free Trade Port:

**Major Pilot FTZs:**
- Shanghai FTZ (2013 - China's first)
- Guangdong FTZ (Shenzhen Qianhai, Guangzhou Nansha, Zhuhai Hengqin)
- Tianjin FTZ
- Fujian FTZ (Xiamen, Fuzhou, Pingtan)
- Liaoning, Zhejiang, Henan, Hubei, Chongqing, Sichuan, Shaanxi FTZs (2017 batch)
- Hainan FTZ / Free Trade Port
- Shandong, Jiangsu, Hebei, Yunnan, Guangxi, Heilongjiang FTZs
- Beijing, Hunan, Anhui FTZs
- Xinjiang FTZ (2023)

In 2024, FTZs attracted **US$28.25 billion in FDI** -- 24.3% of the national total.

### 6.2 Special Customs Supervision Zones (海关特殊监管区域)

**Six types currently exist:**
1. **Bonded Zones** (保税区) - original model for bonded storage and processing
2. **Bonded Logistics Parks** (保税物流园区) - focus on logistics and distribution
3. **Bonded Ports** (保税港区) - port-integrated bonded operations
4. **Export Processing Zones** (出口加工区) - manufacturing for export
5. **Cross-Border Industrial Parks** (跨境工业区) - border area production
6. **Comprehensive Bonded Zones (CBZ)** (综合保税区) - the consolidated model

**Consolidation Trend:** Per a 2015 State Council plan, all zones are being consolidated into **Comprehensive Bonded Zones (CBZs)** as the dominant model. As of 2025, there are over **160 CBZs** nationwide (161 as of 2022 data), dwarfing the 156 bonded zones referenced in Hainan context.

**CBZ Key Policies:**
- Tax refund upon entry (入区即退税)
- Bonded imports (进口保税) -- no duty/VAT until goods enter domestic market
- Free flow of goods within the zone
- Reduced institutional transaction costs

### 6.3 Cross-Border E-Commerce Zones (跨境电子商务综合试验区)

**165 comprehensive CBEC pilot zones** approved by the State Council as of late 2024. These zones support bonded warehouse models for CBEC retail imports, with preferential tax treatment.

### 6.4 Hainan Free Trade Port (海南自由贸易港)

The **world's largest free trade port by area** with a fundamentally different level of openness:

**Key Milestones:**
- 2020: CPC unveiled comprehensive plan to transform Hainan into a strategic hub rivaling Hong Kong, Singapore, and Dubai
- **December 18, 2025:** Island-wide special customs operations launched, making the entire island (35,000+ sq km) a special customs supervision zone -- over 70x the combined area of all 156 existing bonded zones

**Customs Framework ("一线放开，二线管住"):**
- **First Line** (一线, border with rest of world): Freer access for overseas goods
- **Second Line** (二线, border with mainland China): Standard customs controls for goods moving to mainland

**Tax and Tariff Regime:**
- Zero-tariff product coverage expanded from **21% to 74%** (from ~1,900 to over 6,600 items)
- Zero-tariff goods processed in Hainan can be sold to mainland duty-free if local added value >= 30%
- Corporate income tax: **15%** for encouraged industries (vs. 25% nationally)
- Personal income tax: maximum **15%** for qualified talent (vs. 45% nationally, through 2027)
- Duty-free luxury goods shopping for consumers with overseas travel records (annual cap RMB 100,000/person)

**Phased Roadmap:**
- By 2025: Basic policy and institutional framework established
- By 2035: Mature free trade port system
- By mid-century: High-standard free trade port with strong international influence

Since 2020, over **9,600 foreign-invested enterprises** have been established in Hainan.

## 7. REGULATORY AGENCIES (PGA EQUIVALENTS)

### 7.1 GACC (海关总署) - Customs + Former CIQ

Post-2018 merger, GACC handles both traditional customs functions and inspection/quarantine:
- Border clearance and duty collection
- Food safety inspection for imports
- Animal/plant quarantine
- Health quarantine (entry-exit persons)
- Commodity quality inspection
- CCC mark verification at border
- GACC Order 248/249: overseas food manufacturer registration requirements

### 7.2 SAMR (国家市场监督管理总局) - State Administration for Market Regulation

Established in 2018 as a super-regulator overseeing:
- **CNCA** (认证认可监督管理委员会) - Certification and Accreditation Administration
- Product quality and safety supervision
- Standardization (through SAC / 标准化管理委员会)
- Anti-monopoly enforcement
- Oversees **CCC certification** system

### 7.3 CCC - China Compulsory Certification (中国强制性产品认证)

A mandatory safety and quality mark required before certain products can be imported, sold, or used in China:

**Scope:** Electrical products, IT products, consumer appliances, fire safety equipment, auto parts, toys, and expanding categories.

**Process:**
- Application, testing, factory inspection
- Timeline: typically **60-90 days**
- Certificate validity: **5 years** with annual follow-up inspections
- Cost: upward of US$4,500 in fees plus inspector travel
- The CCC mark must be physically applied to individual products

**Recent Expansions (2025-2026):**
- EV power supply equipment: CCC required from March 2025; full compliance deadline August 2026
- Gas appliance safety accessories: effective October 2025
- Lithium-ion batteries and mobile power supplies: effective August 2025
- Seven categories of automotive parts: CCC required from January 2027

**Administering Bodies:** SAMR, CNCA, CQC (China Quality Certification Centre), CCAP (automotive products)

### 7.4 MOFCOM (商务部) - Ministry of Commerce

- Import/export licensing (general and special licenses)
- Trade remedy investigations (anti-dumping, countervailing, safeguards)
- Automatic import license management for monitored goods
- Foreign investment approval
- Export control licensing (dual-use items, strategic materials)
- FTA negotiation and implementation

### 7.5 Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs (农业农村部)

- Agricultural product quality and safety
- Pesticide and veterinary drug regulation
- GMO biosafety management
- Animal disease prevention and quarantine policy

### 7.6 NMPA (国家药品监督管理局) - National Medical Products Administration

Operating under SAMR, NMPA regulates:
- **Medical devices** (including IVDs) - classified into 3 risk categories
- **Pharmaceuticals** - registration and market authorization
- **Cosmetics** - registration (special cosmetics) or notification (general cosmetics)
- Pharmaceutical packaging and ingredients
- Infant formula
- Health food (保健食品)
- Food for special medical purposes

Import, sale, and use of these products requires valid **NMPA registration**.

### 7.8 Standards Framework

China's standards fall into five categories:
- **National Standards (GB / 国家标准)** - mandatory (GB) or voluntary (GB/T)
- Industry Standards (行业标准)
- Local Standards (地方标准)
- Group Standards (团体标准)
- Enterprise Standards (企业标准)

GB standards take precedence. Products must comply with applicable mandatory GB standards.

## 8. CHINA EXPORT CLEARANCE

### 8.1 Export Declaration Requirements

**Legal Framework:** Export Control Law (effective Dec 1, 2020), Foreign Trade Law, and various MOFCOM/GACC implementing regulations.

**Key 2025 Reforms (STA Announcement No. 17 and GACC regulations, effective October 1, 2025):**
- End of third-party declarations: exports must be declared under the exporter's own license
- Mandatory tax registration for exporters of goods subject to VAT or consumption tax
- Dual-title requirement: factories without export licenses must be listed as "production and sales entity" with full details disclosed
- Full traceability across export supply chains
- Invoice records, customs declarations, and foreign exchange receipts must be fully aligned

**Goods Classification:**
- **Permitted goods:** Subject to automatic licensing; license issued upon application, valid up to 6 months
- **Restricted goods:** Require special licenses or are subject to quotas
- **Prohibited goods:** Listed in the Export Prohibited Technology Catalog; cannot be exported

### 8.2 Export Tax Rebate System (出口退税)

China's export VAT rebate system refunds some or all of the VAT and consumption tax paid on exported goods:

**How it works:**
- Rebate rates vary by product, ranging from 0% to 13% (matching the VAT rate for full rebate)
- Exporters must file claims with the State Taxation Administration (国家税务总局)
- Requires aligned documentation: invoices, customs declarations, foreign exchange receipts

**Scale:** By November 2025, cumulative export rebates reached nearly **RMB 2 trillion** (1.9038 trillion yuan), a 5.6% year-on-year increase.

**Major 2026 Policy Changes:**
- Effective April 1, 2026: VAT export rebates **fully eliminated** for **249 categories** of goods, including photovoltaic (PV) products
- Battery products: rebate reduced from 9% to 6% (April 2026 - December 2026), fully removed from January 2027
- Represents one of China's most consequential tax incentive adjustments in over a decade

**Consumption Tax on Exports:** Goods exported from China are generally **exempt** from consumption tax.

### 8.3 Export Licensing and Restrictions

**Export License Categories:**
- Automatic export licenses (自动出口许可) for monitored goods
- Non-automatic export licenses for restricted goods
- Dual-use item export licenses (两用物项出口许可)

**Key Restricted/Controlled Categories:**
- Rare earth elements and processing equipment
- Strategic minerals (gallium, germanium, antimony, graphite)
- Dual-use technologies (nuclear, biological, chemical, missile-related)
- Certain technologies listed in the "Catalogue of Technologies Prohibited or Restricted from Export" (revised July 2025)
- Items on the Dual-Use Items Export Control List

### 8.4 Dual-Use Goods Export Controls (两用物项出口管制)

**Recent Developments (2025):**

**April 2025 (MOFCOM/GAC Announcement No. 18):**
- Added 7 medium/heavy rare earth elements to dual-use export control list: samarium, gadolinium, terbium, dysprosium, lutetium, scandium, yttrium
- Includes metals, alloys, targets, oxides, compounds, mixtures, and permanent magnet materials

**July 2025:**
- Revised "Catalogue of Technologies Prohibited or Restricted from Export"
- Added battery cathode material preparation technology as restricted
- Added 5 additional technologies including spodumene lithium extraction

**October 2025 (MOFCOM/GAC Announcements No. 55-58, 61-62):**
- Export controls on rare earth production equipment and parts
- Controls on certain medium and heavy rare earths
- Controls on rare-earth technologies

**November 2025:** These October controls were **suspended** for one year (until November 10, 2026) pending U.S.-China bilateral negotiations. However, the April 2025 controls remain in effect.

**Extraterritorial Provisions (new in 2025):**
- China's updated rare earth export controls now require export licenses for products **made outside China** if they contain Chinese-origin materials or are produced using Chinese technologies
- 50% rule: if Chinese-origin content exceeds 50%, the product is subject to Chinese export controls regardless of where it is manufactured

**Enforcement Intensification:**
- Administrative enforcement cases rose from 91 to **317 cases** in 2025
- Maximum fines exceeded RMB 1 million for the first time (highest: RMB 6.3417 million)
- Focus areas: dual-use items, strategic minerals, rare earth products, graphite
- Export license review takes up to **45 working days**
- End-user and end-use documentation required for all controlled items
